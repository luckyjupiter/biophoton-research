TABLE 3: Key Findings
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. PMT detection of moderate UPE (10 ph/cm2/s): ~4 hours at 5-sigma
   - Feasible for discovery-level measurements
   - Standard bialkali PMT with 5 cm2 collection area

2. PMT detection of faint UPE (1 ph/cm2/s): ~16 days at 5-sigma
   - Impractical for routine measurements
   - Requires extraordinary stability and systematic control

3. SNSPD provides 1-2 orders of magnitude improvement over PMT
   - Limited by tiny collection area (0.0003 cm2)
   - Prohibitive cost ($100k-$500k)
   - Ideal for definitive measurements if collection optics improved

4. EMCCD with pixel binning is competitive for moderate signals
   - Unique advantage: spatial resolution
   - Excess noise factor (F^2=2) reduces effective QE by sqrt(2)
   - Best path to first high-resolution UPE spectrum

5. SPAD has best timing resolution but tiny collection area
   - Essential for g(2) correlation measurements
   - Collection area limits practical use for UPE detection

6. Coherence detection via g(2) is fundamentally infeasible
   - Multi-mode broadband UPE: g(2)(0) - 1 ~ 10^-6
   - Required integration: >10^16 years
   - Must use alternative approaches (spectral lines, antibunching)

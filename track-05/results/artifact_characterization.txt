====================================================================================================
ARTIFACT CHARACTERIZATION: Sources of False Signal in Biophoton Experiments
====================================================================================================

--- Delayed luminescence ---
  Typical rate:       1000.000 counts/s
  Temporal:           Hyperbolic decay: I(t) ~ t^(-m), m=1-2, seconds to minutes
  Spectral:           Broad, peaks 500-700nm, similar to UPE
  Distinguishable:    Yes
  Mitigation:         Wait 30+ min after light exposure; monitor decay curve

--- Chemiluminescence (oxidative) ---
  Typical rate:       50.000 counts/s
  Temporal:           Steady-state with slow drift; responds to chemical perturbation
  Spectral:           Broad 400-700nm, peaks depend on ROS species
  Distinguishable:    NO - CRITICAL
  Mitigation:         Antioxidant controls; dead tissue comparison

--- Afterpulsing (PMT/SPAD) ---
  Typical rate:       2.000 counts/s
  Temporal:           Exponential delay 20-200ns after parent; creates g2 excess
  Spectral:           Same as dark counts
  Distinguishable:    Yes
  Mitigation:         Characterize AP probability; correct g2; hold-off time

--- Cosmic ray hits ---
  Typical rate:       0.017 counts/s
  Temporal:           Random Poisson; large energy deposit per event
  Spectral:           Broadband; may trigger Cherenkov in glass
  Distinguishable:    Yes
  Mitigation:         Pulse height discrimination; coincidence veto; underground lab

--- Phosphorescence ---
  Typical rate:       10.000 counts/s
  Temporal:           Exponential decay, tau=ms to hours
  Spectral:           Narrow bands, species-specific
  Distinguishable:    Yes
  Mitigation:         Time-resolved measurement; spectral filtering

--- Fluorescence from optics ---
  Typical rate:       5.000 counts/s
  Temporal:           Steady under UV excitation; decays when removed
  Spectral:           Red-shifted from excitation; glass-dependent
  Distinguishable:    Yes
  Mitigation:         Non-fluorescent optics (CaF2, fused silica)

--- Triboluminescence ---
  Typical rate:       100.000 counts/s
  Temporal:           Correlated with mechanical disturbance; burst-like
  Spectral:           Broad visible, often blue-white
  Distinguishable:    Yes
  Mitigation:         Minimize vibration; settle time after handling

--- SPAD cross-talk ---
  Typical rate:       0.010 counts/s
  Temporal:           Coincident with avalanche in neighbor; sub-ns
  Spectral:           NIR 800-1100nm from hot-carrier emission
  Distinguishable:    Yes
  Mitigation:         Optical isolation OD>6; spectral filter

--- Dark rate drift (thermal) ---
  Typical rate:       0.300 counts/s
  Temporal:           Slow monotonic or periodic (room temp cycles)
  Spectral:           None (electronic)
  Distinguishable:    Yes
  Mitigation:         Temp monitoring to 0.01C; interleaved blanks

--- Bioluminescence (enzymatic) ---
  Typical rate:       100.000 counts/s
  Temporal:           Steady state; ATP-dependent
  Spectral:           Narrow 550-570nm (luciferase) or specific
  Distinguishable:    Yes
  Mitigation:         Metabolic inhibitors; spectral analysis


CRITICAL ARTIFACTS (not easily distinguished from true UPE):
------------------------------------------------------------
  * Chemiluminescence (oxidative)
    This artifact has spectral and temporal characteristics
    similar to genuine biophoton emission. Requires careful
    experimental controls to rule out.


RECOMMENDED CONTROL EXPERIMENTS:
------------------------------------------------------------
1. Dead tissue control (heat-killed or chemically fixed)
   - Tests for chemiluminescence from tissue preparation
   - Should show greatly reduced emission
2. Antioxidant treatment (e.g., superoxide dismutase + catalase)
   - Tests for ROS-mediated chemiluminescence
   - Genuine UPE partially blocked; non-ROS emission unaffected
3. Dark adaptation time series (0, 10, 30, 60 min)
   - Tests for delayed luminescence decay
   - True UPE should reach stable baseline
4. Temperature dependence (4C, 25C, 37C)
   - True UPE from live tissue should increase with metabolic rate
   - Chemiluminescence has different temperature dependence
5. Spectral analysis (broadband filters at minimum)
   - Different sources have different spectral signatures
   - At least 3 wavelength bands recommended
6. Mechanical vibration test
   - Rules out triboluminescence
7. Blank control (buffer/medium without tissue)
   - Rules out contamination from mounting materials

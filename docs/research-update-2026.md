# Biophoton Research Update — February 2026

## 1. Liu et al. 2024 — Entangled Biphoton Generation in Myelin Sheath

**Citation:** Liu Z, Chen Y-C, Ao P. "Entangled biphoton generation in myelin sheath." *Physical Review E* 110, 024402 (2024). arXiv: 2401.11682.

**Status:** Published August 2024 in Physical Review E (a serious physics journal, not a fringe outlet).

**Key findings:**
- Myelin sheath cylindrical cavities can theoretically generate entangled photon pairs via cavity QED
- C-H bond vibrations in lipid tails serve as the photon source through cascade radiation
- Peak entanglement at biologically realistic myelin thicknesses (g-ratio ~0.8, myelin 0.8–1.1 μm)
- Entanglement decreases rapidly as myelin thins — consistent with neurodegenerative disease patterns

**Citation count:** As of Feb 2026, appears to have modest but growing citations on Semantic Scholar. The paper generated discussion on Reddit r/neuro (Aug 2024) and was picked up by Synthical. No published rebuttals or critiques found, but also no experimental follow-up yet.

**Assessment:** Theoretically rigorous but experimentally untested. The prediction that demyelination reduces entangled photon production is elegant but detection remains technically marginal.

## 2. Major New Publications (2024–2026)

### 2a. "Exploring ultraweak photon emissions as optical markers of brain activity" — iScience (Feb 2025)
- Proposes "photoencephalography" — using UPE as a brain imaging modality
- Shows brain UPE spectra and entropy vary by task, diverging from background
- Optical readouts correlate with evoked neuroelectric oscillations
- Key advance: moving from "biophotons exist" to "biophotons carry functional information"

### 2b. Erboz et al. "Electromagnetic radiation and biophoton emission in neuronal communication and neurodegenerative diseases" — Prog Biophys Mol Biol (2025)
- Comprehensive review linking biophotons to neurodegenerative disease
- Analyzes dual signaling (electrochemical + biophotonic)
- Explores oxidative stress → altered biophoton emission in Alzheimer's/Parkinson's
- Discusses Low-Level Laser Therapy (LLLT) for modulating biophoton activity

### 2c. Nevoit et al. (2024–2025) — Biophotonic signaling conceptualization
- Series of papers building a working model of biophoton signaling at cell, intercellular matrix, and whole-organism levels
- Published in Frontiers in Systems Neuroscience (2025)
- Represents the most ambitious attempt to date at a unified biophotonic signaling framework

### 2d. Frede, Zadeh-Haghighi & Simon (2023→2024)
- Published in IEEE J. Selected Topics in Quantum Electronics (2024)
- Polarization well preserved through multiple Nodes of Ranvier
- Transmission losses approximately multiplicative (not catastrophic)
- Strengthens case for both classical and quantum photonic communication

## 4. Has Anyone Measured Biophotons in Demyelination Models?

**Answer: No.** As of February 2026, no published study has specifically measured biophoton emission in a demyelination model (EAE, cuprizone, lysolecithin, or patient tissue). This remains the central research gap.

**Closest existing work:**
- Wang et al. 2023: Showed reduced biophotonic activity + spectral blueshift in Alzheimer's and vascular dementia models — both conditions involve white matter degeneration, but demyelination was not the primary focus
- Chen et al. 2020: Aging mouse brain spectral blueshift — aging involves myelin thinning
- Zangari et al. 2021: Detected photons at nodes of Ranvier in active nerves — but in healthy tissue

**The opportunity is wide open.**

## 5. Remyelination Therapy Updates (2024–2026)

### Clemastine + Metformin Combination
- **CCMR-Two trial** (Phase 2, presented at ECTRIMS 2025, Sept 2025)
- Combining metformin and clemastine "significantly increased myelin repair" in RRMS patients
- However: effects were small; no improvements in disability or visual function after 6 months
- Third trial showing significant remyelination effect from this combo
- May need longer-term trials — remyelination → disability reduction could take years
- Lead: Nick Cunniffe, University of Cambridge

### Clemastine Concerns
- A 2024 paper raised concerns: "Clemastine fumarate accelerates accumulation of disability in progressive multiple sclerosis by enhancing pyroptosis"
- Suggests clemastine may be beneficial in RRMS but harmful in progressive MS
- Important nuance for the field

### Convelo Therapeutics — New Pipeline
- Inhibiting CYP51 or EBP to promote OPC differentiation
- IND applications expected 2025–2026
- Novel mechanism distinct from clemastine

### NeurologyLive Pipeline Review (Feb 2026)
- Multiple agents in various trial stages
- Overall: remyelination is now a real therapeutic target, not just theory
- But no agent yet shows robust clinical improvement — measuring remyelination remains the bottleneck

## 6. Competing/Complementary Hypotheses

### Electromagnetic Field (EMF) Hypothesis
- Neurons generate EM fields detectable by EEG/MEG
- Some argue these fields themselves carry information (ephaptic coupling)
- Complementary to biophoton hypothesis — different frequency range

### Quantum Brain Hypotheses
- Penrose-Hameroff orchestrated objective reduction (microtubules)
- Fisher's quantum cognition (Posner molecules, nuclear spin)
- Biophoton/myelin waveguide hypothesis is more modest and testable than these

### Mitochondrial Signaling
- Biophotons are largely generated by mitochondrial ROS
- Some researchers see biophotons as purely metabolic byproducts, not signals
- Key debate: byproduct vs. signal is not settled

### Cifra et al. 2015 Critical Review
- Showed no reliable evidence for coherence or nonclassicality of biophoton emission
- Important intellectual check — the field needs rigor, not wishful thinking
- Does NOT disprove the waveguide hypothesis (which is about propagation, not source coherence)

## 7. Key Takeaways for Podcast

1. The field is accelerating — 2024–2025 saw more papers than previous years combined
2. Liu et al. 2024 is the most theoretically ambitious paper (entangled photons in myelin)
3. The iScience 2025 "photoencephalography" paper is the boldest experimental proposal
4. **Nobody has done the demyelination experiment** — this is the gap
5. Remyelination therapies are real but measuring success remains the bottleneck
6. The biophoton community and the neuroimmunology community have not yet connected
7. Detection technology (SNSPDs, EM-CCDs) is ready — the experiments are feasible now
